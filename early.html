Early Society

Learn about the early days of the Order of Gimghoul

The Facts

  • In 1889, the Order of Dromgoole was founded by UNC students, inspired by other college secret societies like Yale's Skull and Bones.
  • The society was named after Peter Dromgoole, a student who mysteriously disappeared in the 1830s.
  • It was later renamed the Order of the Gimghoul, reflecting a theme of midnight, graves, and weirdness.
  • Membership was exclusive to male students of junior class or higher and faculty, with early members' names published in yearbooks.
  • The Order of the Gimghoul, distinguished by owning a castle, is among other campus societies like the Gorgon's Head and the Golden Fleece.
  • In 1915, the Order acquired land near campus, part of which became Battle Park and part the site for their castle, completed in 1926.
  • The castle, known as Hippol Castle, was designed by architect and UNC alumnus Nathaniel Cortlandt Curtis.
